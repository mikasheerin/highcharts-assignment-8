Hate Crimes and Race in the United States

By Mikaela Sheerin on October 20, 2017

Of all the hate crimes committed in the United States in 2015, 56.9% are motivated by racial biases, according to the FBI - a larger percentage than all other biases, like religion or sexual orientation, combined. Of the offenses that involve racial biases, 52.7% are motivated by anti-black or anti-African American sentiment. 150 years after the U.S. abolished slavery and 50 years after the Civil Rights movement, data show Black Americans still face a disproportionate threat of hate-related violence, lending credibility to the Black Lives Matter credo: in the eyes of many Americans, black lives really do matter less.
